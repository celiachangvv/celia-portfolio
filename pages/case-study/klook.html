Klook

UI/UX Design

Rebranding

Case Study

Klook is a website and app that provides travel tickets and guides to users, making it easy for them to purchase tickets and enjoy their trip.

Insight

Overview

Klook is a travel app that allows users to easily plan travel itineraries. They offer a variety of options for tickets, tickets, airport transfers, hotels, etc. so that users can enjoy their trip.

Problem

On the Klook website, the interface is somewhat cluttered because they want to showcase elements beyond the main content, such as partnerships, and so on. Additionally, the card components lack consistency. I understand that this is likely intended to differentiate various themes, but an excessive number of components can be overwhelming for users.

Analysis

Branding

In Klook's logo, five different colors are used to represent their brand's attributes. These shapes resemble party streamers, suggesting that Klook wants to convey qualities such as 'joy,' 'dynamic nature,' 'excitement,' and 'optimism.' Additionally, the spacing between these colors forms the letter 'K.

UI Kit

Typography

Klook uses 'Poppins' for its typography because 'Poppins' is known for its clean and readable design, conveying a modern, approachable, and user-friendly image. It's well-suited for a travel app, making it easier for users to engage with the application.

Color Palette
Icon

While Klook's icon colors are diverse, I believe that these colors are all in harmony with the color palette and are consistent with the qualities that Klook represents: 'joy,' 'dynamic nature,' 'excitement,' and 'optimism.' The unique icon design also allows users to experience the pleasure of traveling. Just as Klook aims to bring joy to its users through its brand, looking at these uniquely designed icons also further attracts users to use the app.
